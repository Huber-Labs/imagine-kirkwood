# Imagine Kirkwood Image Style Guide

## Purpose

Concept images should help residents imagine a better Kirkwood Avenue before they are asked to evaluate ideas.

The goal is not fantasy. The goal is optimistic realism: a future that feels beautiful, civic, human-scaled, and possible.

## Core Visual Direction

Images should feel like a polished urban design studio visualization for a real civic project.

References:
- Gehl Architects
- Urban Land Institute
- high-quality architecture competition boards
- Apple/Airbnb editorial warmth
- college-town main street photography

## Place Character

Always preserve the feeling of Bloomington, Indiana:

- limestone architecture
- historic college-town storefronts
- mature trees
- Indiana University context
- warm brick, stone, and wood tones
- human-scale streets
- local businesses rather than generic chains

## People and Public Life

Show the street as actively used, but not overcrowded.

Include a natural mix of:

- students
- families
- older adults
- people with strollers
- cyclists
- people using mobility devices
- outdoor diners
- musicians or small performances when appropriate

The mood should be welcoming, comfortable, and civic.

## Urban Design Elements

Use believable, practical improvements:

- wider sidewalks
- outdoor dining
- shade trees
- movable chairs and tables
- planters
- rain gardens
- raised crossings
- bike parking
- warm pedestrian lighting
- subtle string lights
- public art
- traffic-calming elements
- flexible market or festival space

Avoid futuristic or unrealistic elements.

## Lighting and Mood

Preferred mood:

- warm late-afternoon or early-evening light
- soft shadows
- inviting atmosphere
- clear visibility
- calm optimism

Avoid:
- harsh midday glare
- overly dramatic cyberpunk lighting
- empty streets
- dystopian or chaotic scenes

## Composition

Use wide landscape images, ideally 16:9.

The image should work as a website hero image.

Prioritize:
- clear street perspective
- recognizable public realm
- strong foreground activity
- comfortable human scale
- visible storefronts
- enough open space for the scene to breathe

Avoid:
- excessive text/signage
- distorted buildings
- unreadable labels
- impossible road geometry
- too many cars dominating the scene
